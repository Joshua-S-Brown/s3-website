Solverde

Where the green sun meets the sea

◎ Interactive town map · coming soon

The town rises from the shore of Azure Cove in layers of whitewash and terracotta, built at a scale that makes the word "town" generous and the word "village" unfair. Solverde sits on the inner curve of a C-shaped cove on the Cerulean Sea, sheltered by a living reef stretched between two peninsulas. The water inside the cove is calm enough to see the bottom on a good day. The water outside is not.

Everything here is built from what the giants left behind and what the land provides. Walls are driftwood frames plastered with clay and sand, whitewashed with paste made from crushed shells. Roofs are terracotta tile, scavenged or hand-fired. Windows are bottle-bottoms and sea glass set in salvaged frames. Floors are mosaic, assembled from fragments of tile nobody remembers choosing but everyone agrees are beautiful. Every home has one piece of salvage from the old world scaled to remind you how big the giants were. A doorknob used as a table. A coin framed on a wall like a shield.

The name comes from old human resort signage the animals adopted. The humans meant it as marketing. The animals made it literally true. Sol verde. Green sun. Walk up from the harbor at the right hour and the light through the canopy turns everything the color the name promised.
